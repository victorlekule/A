SAUTI YA AUTISM PROJECT

The "Comfort for Child Life" was the first major project under the project of SAUTI YA AUTISM undertaken by our founders, and it served as the catalyst for the formation and registration of the African Child Comforters Organization (ACCO) in 2024.

What began as a youth-led fundraising campaign in November 2021 by students at Ndwika Girls Secondary School , rapidly grew into a mission to support the most vulnerable: children with autism.

The goal was to address the critical lack of proper sleeping facilities at Lulindi Maalumu School(special school) in Masasi District, Mtwara Region, Tanzania, where students often faced inadequate resources and heightened vulnerability

Lives Transformed Over 400 people participated in this foundational initiative, driving a powerful social and physical transformation. This massive gathering successfully united a diverse group of key stakeholders from parents and teachers to government leaders, religious figures, and dispensary nurses for a vital, hot discussion on autism awareness and solutions to combat stigma. This successful dialogue initiated a major cultural shift toward community inclusion and support.

Simultaneously, the effort secured and delivered 10 new beds to the students at Lulindi Maalumu School(Special school), matching the new social hope with immediate physical dignity and

The community speak/Testimonial: "The beds provided by this dedicated group have significantly improved our students’ well-being and restored dignity. But it is the courageous awareness dialogue which brought together parents, leaders, and teachers that has truly given us hope for an inclusive future for our children."

Madam Sauda Hamidu Kalanje, Head of Lulindi Maalumu Primary School

About Us

The African Child Comforters Organisation (ACCO) originated in 2021 as a student support group, primarily focused on giving back to vulnerable communities in Mtwara Tanzania.

A group of dedicated students pooled their pocket money to assist local initiatives, which included providing sanitary pads to secondary school pupils, furnishing primary schools with desks, and promoting environmental protection programs in educational institutions.
