OUR PROJECT

We aim to touch the lives of underprivileged children through various initiatives focused on education, health, and overall well-being.

THE VALENTINE'S CHARITY PROJECT

The Valentine’s Charity event was organized jointly by the African Child Comforters Organization (ACCO) and Elite Trial on 16th February 2025 at Christ Hope Bugando (City Center), Mwanza, Tanzania. The purpose of the event was to celebrate Valentine’s Day through charitable giving by providing school and hygiene supplies to 50 children under the care of the center, which supports orphans and children living with HIV/AIDS.

DAMU SALAMA BUGANDO PROJECT

On January 11, 2025, the African Child Comforters Organisation (ACCO) in collaboration with Damu Salama Bugando conducted a blood donation exercise at the St. Augustine University of Tanzania (SAUT). The main purpose of this event was to increase the supply of safe blood for patients in need—especially children, pregnant women, and emergency patients—in Bugando Hospital and other health centers in the Lake Zone.

The NURU YA BINT BROJECT

The Nuru ya Binti project, organized by the African Child Comforters Organization (ACCO), is a one-day empowerment program scheduled for 28th May 2025 at Nyasubi Primary School (Mwanza) and Gongo la Mboto JICA Primary School (Dar es Salaam). The initiative aims to support schoolgirls aged 10–18 by addressing menstrual health and gender-based violence (GBV) challenges that hinder their education and wellbeing.

SAUTI YA AUTISM PROJECT

The "Comfort for Child Life" was the first major project under the project of SAUTI YA AUTISM undertaken by our founders, and it served as the catalyst for the formation and registration of the African Child Comforters Organization (ACCO) in 2024.

What began as a youth-led fundraising campaign in November 2021 by students at Ndwika Girls Secondary School , rapidly grew into a mission to support the most vulnerable: children with autism.

About Us

The African Child Comforters Organisation (ACCO) originated in 2021 as a student support group, primarily focused on giving back to vulnerable communities in Mtwara Tanzania.

A group of dedicated students pooled their pocket money to assist local initiatives, which included providing sanitary pads to secondary school pupils, furnishing primary schools with desks, and promoting environmental protection programs in educational institutions.
