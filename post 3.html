The Nuru ya Binti

The Nuru ya Binti project, organized by the African Child Comforters Organization (ACCO), is a one-day empowerment program scheduled for 28th May 2025 at Nyasubi Primary School (Mwanza) and Gongo la Mboto JICA Primary School (Dar es Salaam). The initiative aims to support schoolgirls aged 10–18 by addressing menstrual health and gender-based violence (GBV) challenges that hinder their education and wellbeing.

ACCO, a youth-led non-profit registered under the NGO Act No. 24 of 2002, focuses on improving children’s lives through education, health, and advocacy. The Nuru ya Binti project—meaning “Light of the Girl in School”—seeks to empower girls through menstrual education, pad distribution, and GBV awareness.

Key activities include distributing sanitary pads, teaching menstrual hygiene and reproductive health, and conducting sessions on GBV awareness and self-protection. These sessions will be delivered through interactive methods such as group discussions, storytelling, and role plays, guided by trained facilitators from the Gender Desk.

Expected outcomes include improved menstrual hygiene, reduced school absenteeism, greater awareness of GBV, and stronger community support systems.

In conclusion, Nuru ya Binti is a vital intervention that promotes dignity, confidence, and equal educational opportunities for girls, reinforcing ACCO’s mission to create a caring and inclusive environment for all children.

About Us

The African Child Comforters Organisation (ACCO) originated in 2021 as a student support group, primarily focused on giving back to vulnerable communities in Mtwara Tanzania.

A group of dedicated students pooled their pocket money to assist local initiatives, which included providing sanitary pads to secondary school pupils, furnishing primary schools with desks, and promoting environmental protection programs in educational institutions.
